// 01 MARKET STRUCTURE & SMC

Breaking Market Cycles

Market Structure is the backbone of Price Action. Professional institutional traders ignore indicators and look for BOS (Break of Structure) and CHoCH (Change of Character).

Uptrend (Bullish)

Characterized by Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL). The trend remains intact as long as HL holds.

Downtrend (Bearish)

Characterized by Lower Highs (LH) and Lower Lows (LL). The trend remains intact as long as LH holds.

// 02 WYCKOFF THEORY

Tracking Smart Money (Institutions)

Richard Wyckoff's theory is the study of Supply and Demand and how institutions manipulate price. There are four phases: Accumulation (Buying), Markup (Pumping), Distribution (Selling), and Markdown (Crashing).

Spring: A final trap where price breaks support to trigger stop losses before reversing hard.
Upthrust (UTAD): A fake breakout above resistance designed to trap retail buyers before the crash.

// THE MASTER GLOSSARY (A-Z)

Arbitrage

Risk-free profit by buying in one market and selling in another.

Bear Trap

A signal that suggests a rising trend is reversing, but it's a trap.

Dead Cat Bounce

A temporary recovery after a massive market crash.

Greeks

Mathematical tools (Delta, Gamma, Theta) to measure option risk.

Hedge

An investment made to reduce the risk of adverse price movements.

Liquidity

How quickly an asset can be sold without causing a price move.

Margin Call

When a broker demands more cash because your position is losing too much.

Open Interest

Total number of outstanding derivative contracts.

Scalping

A style of trading where you take small profits on many trades.
